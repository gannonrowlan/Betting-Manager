# Betting-Manager

Bankroll IQ
A performance and discipline app for sports bettors.
Core promise
“Know whether you are actually winning, where your edge is, and how to manage your bankroll without guessing.”
That is much stronger than “AI picks.”
What features should be in version 1
Start with only the features that make someone say, “I need this every week.”
MVP features
manual bet entry
bankroll balance
unit size setting
profit/loss tracking
win rate
ROI
record by sport
record by bet type
odds history
dashboard with filters
weekly/monthly recap
notes/tags on bets
Version 2
CLV tracking
import from sportsbooks by CSV
tax estimate summary
streak and variance graphs
alerts when bet size breaks bankroll rules
“what is your actual edge?” reports
Version 3
automatic syncing with books
line shopping
social sharing
community leaderboards
sharp bettor workflow tools
The biggest mistake to avoid
Do not build it around “winning picks.”
That creates problems:
way harder to prove value
more trust issues
more competition
more policy and legal risk
users cancel fast if your picks go cold
Tracking apps are better because they are useful even when users lose. They keep paying for insight, organization, and discipline.
How to charge monthly
Subscription can work well here because analytics are an ongoing service, and both Apple and Google support recurring in-app subscriptions. Apple’s auto-renewable subscriptions are specifically built for ongoing premium features and services.
A clean pricing model:
Free: up to 25 bets per month, basic dashboard
Pro: $7.99 to $12.99/month
Pro+: $15.99 to $24.99/month if you add sync/import, CLV, exports, and deeper analytics
What people would actually pay for:
unlimited tracking
sportsbook imports
CLV reports
tax summaries
advanced charts
exports
bankroll coaching rules
